The Steam-Powered City The Brass City a place of steam and smoke, where towering machines belch black clouds into the sky. The clanging of gears and the hissing of steam fills the air, as workers toil in the factories and mills. The stench of burning coal and the taste of ash on the tongue are constant reminders of the power of steam, driving them ever forward into an industrial future.

Summary

  • The Arcadia is a place of great wealth and poverty
  • Industry and progress come at a high cost to laborers
  • Political power is decentralized, with various groups vying for control
  • Guards are privately hired and serve the interests of their employers

Social Classes

  • Upper class lives in inner districts
  • Middle class lives in outer districts
  • Lower class lives either in the tenement districts or in the slums

Economy

  • Large Whaling industry
  • Automated manufacturing of several goods, most exported to the continent
  • Smaller Free States outposts provide raw goods that are shipped to the city
  • Growing weapons industry, providing Altonia and Mire weapons for their conflict

Politics

  • ‘Ruled’ by a council of wealthy industrialists, bankers, merchants, etc
  • Inner council of 5 or so most influential people in the city
  • Outer council of about 100 or so members

Dwarven Exiles

  • The Exiles are a group of dwarves who live in the Free States
  • They were outcast from dwarven society for various reasons, including going against the traditional dwarven taboo against automation
  • The Free States is a welcoming environment for the Exiles, where they work as craftsmen, blacksmiths, and engineers
  • The Exiles have embraced steam-powered technology and used it to improve traditional dwarven crafts
  • They are a tight-knit community, bonded by shared experiences of rejection and discrimination
  • The Exiles' contributions to the Free States' cultures and traditions are valued and appreciated
  • For many dwarves, the Free States may be seen as anathema due to its industrialization and automation, but for the Exiles it is a place of opportunity and a chance to make their own way in the world
