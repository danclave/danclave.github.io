The Kingdom of Mire

A harsh land filled with mystery, danger, and supernatural horrors

  • Most of the kingdom is made up of inhospitable swamps with deadly creatures and malevolent spirits
  • The people are hardy and resilient, mostly poor farmers and fishermen who rely on the swamp's bounty to survive
  • The few cities are small and cramped, with narrow streets and humble buildings
  • The people are fiercely loyal to their kingdom and king, determined to survive and thrive in the face of dangers

The Swamp

  • Home to various deadly creatures and malevolent spirits
  • Said to be full of hidden treasures and ancient relics waiting to be discovered
  • A place of great danger and mystery, where only the brave or foolhardy dare to venture

The Church of the Light

  • Dominant religion in the Kingdom of Mire
  • Hierarchical structure with a central authority (High Priest) and council of bishops and priests
  • Strong following among the population and its teachings and traditions are deeply ingrained in the culture of the kingdom
  • Strict moral code and opposition to other religions and belief systems

The Bog-Touched

  • Various sects and cults that exist in Mire, including those that make pacts with or worship supernatural swamp entities
  • Viewed with suspicion and fear by the Church and the general population
  • Targeted by the Church's crusades and persecutions
  • Despite Church efforts, these beliefs persist in Mire, hidden in shadows and lurking in dark corners of the swamp

Names for the People of Mire

  • "Swamp Rats" (derogatory)
  • "Marshmen"
  • "Mireborn"
  • "Mirefolk"
  • Bogmen

The Well

  • Mythical place in the swamps of Mire, whispered about in hushed tones
  • Said to be a bottomless well leading to the depths of hell
  • Origins unknown, said to have been created by a madman seeking power or a group of cultists seeking to commune with dark forces of the underworld
  • A place of great danger and malevolence, guarded by demonic creatures or a malevolent spirit
  • A place to be avoided at all costs
